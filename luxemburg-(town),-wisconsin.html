Luxemburg (town), Wisconsin

Luxemburg is a town in Kewaunee County, Wisconsin, United States. The population was 1,469 at the 2010 census. The village of Luxemburg, a separate municipality, is surrounded by the town. The unincorporated communities of Walhain and Casco Junction are in the town, and Neuern, Frog Station, and Tonet are partially in the town.





== Geography ==

Luxemburg is on the western side of Kewaunee County, bordered to the west by Brown County. According to the United States Census Bureau, the town has a total area of 34.0 square miles (88.1 km2), all of it land.





== Demographics ==

As of the census of 2000, there were 1,402 people, 445 households, and 383 families residing in the town. The population density was 41.0 people per square mile (15.8/km2). There were 459 housing units at an average density of 13.4 per square mile (5.2/km2). The racial makeup of the town was 98.86% White, 0.14% Black or African American, 0.07% Asian, 0.50% from other races, and 0.43% from two or more races.
