Menomonie (town), Wisconsin

Menomonie is a town in Dunn County, Wisconsin, United States. The population was 3,174 at the 2000 census. The unincorporated community of Irvington lies within the town, as does most of the City of Menomonie.





== Geography ==

According to the United States Census Bureau, the town has a total area of 42.0 square miles (108.8 km2), of which, 41.8 square miles (108.3 km2) of it is land and 0.2 square miles (0.5 km2) of it (0.50%) is water.





== Demographics ==



As of the census of 2000, there were 3,174 people, 1,129 households, and 871 families residing in the town. The population density was 75.9 people per square mile (29.3/km2). There were 1,166 housing units at an average density of 27.9 per square mile (10.8/km2). The racial makeup of the town was 92.25% White, 0.09% Black or African American, 0.13% Native American, 6.81% Asian, 0.19% from other races, and 0.54% from two or more races. 0.54% of the population were Hispanic or Latino of any race.

There were 1,129 households, out of which 37.5% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 67.1% were married couples living together, 6.6% had a female householder with no husband present, and 22.8% were non-families.
