Shullsburg (town), Wisconsin

Shullsburg is a town in Lafayette County, Wisconsin, United States. The population was 354 at the 2010 census. The city of Shullsburg is located within the town but is separate from it. The unincorporated community of Dunbarton is located partially in the town.





== History ==

Shullsburg was established in 1827. The town was named after Jesse W. Shull. His mine is now part of the Badger Mine and Museum, a tourist attraction.





== Geography ==

The town is in southern Lafayette County, southwest of Darlington, the county seat. According to the United States Census Bureau, the town of Shullsburg has a total area of 34.7 square miles (90.0 km2), all of it land.





== Demographics ==

As of the census of 2000, there were 364 people, 136 households, and 102 families residing in the town.
