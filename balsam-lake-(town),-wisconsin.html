Balsam Lake (town), Wisconsin

Balsam Lake is a town in Polk County, Wisconsin, United States. The population was 1,384 at the 2000 census. The Village of Balsam Lake is located within the town. The unincorporated community of Lykens is also located in the town.





== Geography ==

According to the United States Census Bureau, the town has a total area of 32.2 square miles (83.5 km2), of which, 29.7 square miles (77.0 km2) of it is land and 2.5 square miles (6.5 km2) of it (7.76%) is water.





== Demographics ==

As of the census of 2000, there were 1,384 people, 529 households, and 402 families residing in the town. The population density was 46.6 people per square mile (18.0/km2). There were 1,018 housing units at an average density of 34.2 per square mile (13.2/km2). The racial makeup of the town was 96.89% White, 0.29% Black or African American, 1.37% Native American, 0.72% Asian, 0.07% from other races, and 0.65% from two or more races. 0.58% of the population were Hispanic or Latino of any race.
