Baldwin (town), Wisconsin

Baldwin is a town in St. Croix County, Wisconsin, United States. The population was 903 at the 2000 census. The Village of Baldwin is located partially within the town. The unincorporated community of Dahl is also located in the town.





== Geography ==

According to the United States Census Bureau, the town has a total area of 32.2 square miles (83.3 km2), of which, 32.1 square miles (83.1 km2) of it is land and 0.1 square miles (0.2 km2) of it (0.25%) is water.





== Demographics ==



As of the census of 2000, there were 903 people, 307 households, and 272 families residing in the town. The population density was 28.1 inhabitants per square mile (10.8/km2). There were 315 housing units at an average density of 9.8 per square mile (3.8/km2). The racial makeup of the town was 97.23% White, 0.11% Native American, 1.33% Asian, 0.22% from other races, and 1.11% from two or more races.
