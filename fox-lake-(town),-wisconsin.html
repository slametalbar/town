Fox Lake (town), Wisconsin

Fox Lake is a town in Dodge County, Wisconsin, United States. The population was 2,402 at the 2000 census. The City of Fox Lake is located within the town. The unincorporated communities of Delbern Acres, Fox Lake Junction, and Lyndon Dale are also located in the town.





== History ==

Fox Lake is derived from the Indian name of the Lake, hosh a rac ah tah, meaning "fox".





== Geography ==

According to the United States Census Bureau, the town has a total area of 38.2 square miles (99.0 km2), of which, 32.2 square miles (83.5 km2) of it is land and 6.0 square miles (15.4 km2) of it (15.60%) is water.

The 2,713 acre lake also known as Fox Lake is found within the town.





== Demographics ==

As of the census of 2000, there were 2,402 people, 477 households, and 341 families residing in the town. The population density was 74.5 people per square mile (28.8/km2). There were 897 housing units at an average density of 27.8 per square mile (10.7/km2).
