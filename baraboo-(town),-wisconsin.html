Baraboo (town), Wisconsin

Baraboo is a town in Sauk County, Wisconsin, United States. The population was 1,816 at the 2020 census. The City of Baraboo is located mostly within the town. The unincorporated community of Crawford Crossing is located in the town.





== Geography ==

According to the United States Census Bureau, the town has a total area of 32.6 square miles (84 km2), of which, 32.0 square miles (83 km2) of it is land and 0.6 square miles (1.6 km2) of it (1.69%) is water.





== Demographics ==

As of the census of 2000, there were 1,828 people, 685 households, and 500 families residing in the town. The population density was 57.1 people per square mile (22.0 people/km2). There were 751 housing units at an average density of 23.5 units per square mile (9.1 units/km2). The racial makeup of the town was 97.48% White, 0.22% Black or African American, 0.82% Native American, 0.27% Asian, 0.05% Pacific Islander, 0.55% from other races, and 0.60% from two or more races. 1.59% of the population were Hispanic or Latino of any race.
