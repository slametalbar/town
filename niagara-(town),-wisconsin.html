Niagara (town), Wisconsin

Niagara is a town in Marinette County, Wisconsin, United States. The population was 924 at the 2000 census. The city of Niagara is located to the north of and adjacent to the town.





== Geography ==

According to the United States Census Bureau, the town has a total area of 68.4 square miles (177.1 km2), of which, 67.1 square miles (173.8 km2) of it is land and 1.2 square miles (3.2 km2) of it (1.83%) is water.





== Demographics ==

As of the census of 2000, there were 924 people, 348 households, and 263 families residing in the town. The population density was 13.8 inhabitants per square mile (5.3/km2). There were 453 housing units at an average density of 6.8 per square mile (2.6/km2). The racial makeup of the town was 98.16% White, 0.32% Black or African American, 0.32% Native American, 0.43% Asian, 0.32% from other races, and 0.43% from two or more races. 1.08% of the population were Hispanic or Latino of any race.

There were 348 households, out of which 37.4% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 68.1% were married couples living together, 3.4% had a female householder with no husband present, and 24.4% were non-families.
