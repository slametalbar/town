Solon Springs (town), Wisconsin

Solon Springs is a town in Douglas County, Wisconsin, United States. As of the 2010 census, the town had a total population of 910. The Village of Solon Springs is located within the town. The unincorporated community of Sauntry is also located in the town.





== Geography ==

According to the United States Census Bureau, the town has a total area of 84.5 square miles (218.9 km2), of which, 83.0 square miles (215.0 km2) of it is land and 1.5 square miles (3.8 km2) of it (1.75%) is water.

Solon Springs is located 8 miles north of Gordon, 63 miles west southwest of the city of Ashland and 32 miles southeast of the city of Superior. Solon Springs is located on the west shoreline of the Upper Saint Croix Lake, the headwaters of the St. Croix River.





== Transportation ==

U.S. Highway 53, Business 53, and County Hwy A serve as a main routes in the community.





== Demographics ==

As of the census of 2000, there were 807 people, 334 households, and 241 families residing in the town.
