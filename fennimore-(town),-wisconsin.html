Fennimore (town), Wisconsin

Fennimore is a town in Grant County, Wisconsin, United States. The population was 599 at the 2000 census. The size of the town was reduced when Mount Ida was split off, and when the City of Fennimore was incorporated from a part of the town. The unincorporated community of Lancaster Junction, Wisconsin is located in the town. The unincorporated community of Preston is also located partially in the town.





== History ==

Fennimore was named for a settler who disappeared during the Black Hawk War.





== Geography ==

According to the United States Census Bureau, the town has a total area of 34.8 square miles (90.0 km2), all of it land.





== Demographics ==

As of the census of 2000, there were 599 people, 199 households, and 157 families residing in the town. The population density was 17.2 people per square mile (6.7/km2). There were 216 housing units at an average density of 2.4 persons/km2 (6.2 persons/sq mi).
